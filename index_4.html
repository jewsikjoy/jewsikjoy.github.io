01 — About

From substations to smart grids

I am a PhD student in the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering at Prairie View A&M University, Texas. My research develops optimization methods for modern power systems — from optimal power flow under uncertainty to the control and management of microgrids with high renewable penetration.

Before my doctoral studies, I earned an M.Sc. in Energy Technology from Chittagong University of Engineering and Technology, where my thesis designed a hybrid renewable energy management system using artificial-intelligence techniques, and a B.Sc. in Electrical & Electronic Engineering from International Islamic University Chittagong.

Between my degrees, I spent six years in industry — commissioning water-treatment plants, planning electrical and mechanical systems for a metro rail station, and co-founding a control-panel design firm. That experience keeps my research grounded in how real infrastructure is built and operated.
